How does the practitioner know my remedy is working?
In assessing clients’ reactions to constitutional remedies, homeopaths are guided by Hering’s Law of Cure, named after Dr. Constantine Hering, an American homeopath. Its first and most reliable principle is that healing proceeds from the inside out, from the most important internal organs out to the skin. Even before you notice a measurable difference in your physical symptoms, you may experience an increase in well-being, optimism, and energy within days or weeks of taking a well-chosen remedy. The mind is of central importance, and this is a signal that healing has begun from within.

The second principle of Hering’s Law of Cure is that healing proceeds from the top down. Although not always true in practice, stiffness and pain in the upper body may lessen before improvement is noticed in the lower extremities and joints. You may find your headaches improve, while your knee pain remains the same or temporarily worsens before later improving.

The final principle is that symptoms disappear in reverse order of their appearance. If you had severe eczema as a baby, then developed bronchitis, then asthma, you usually find your asthma symptoms improve first. You may then develop a bronchial cough which, if it runs its course without antibiotic suppression, will no longer be a common winter occurrence. Old skin symptoms of eczema may then reappear. If cortisone and similar suppressive treatments are avoided, your body will eventually heal itself of this more superficial, although unpleasant, symptom. In the meantime, you enjoy heightened energy and well-being, as well as overall better health and ease in your daily life.


How is homeopathy different from naturopathy?
Both homeopaths and naturopaths study physical and medical sciences, including anatomy and pathology. High quality training programs are about four years in length and include professional ethics and supervised clinical experience with clients. In addition, naturopaths study homeopathy, acupuncture, nutrition, herbalism, and a variety of other healing modalities. By contrast, homeopaths study in depth a wide variety of remedies (Materia Medica), repertory, homeopathic philosophy and history, miasmatic theory, case-taking, case analysis, and case management. To use a medical analogy, a naturopath is somewhat similar to a family practitioner or internist, and a homeopath is somewhat similar to a specialist.


Do I have to give up coffee and peppermint toothpaste?
Coffee (either regular or decaf) in moderation (1-2 cups per day) does not usually interfere with the action of homeopathic remedies. If you are very sensitive to coffee, you may want to limit its use for reasons other than its possible interference with homeopathy. If needed, remedies can be taken daily in liquid form by people who drink a lot of coffee. Peppermint-flavored toothpaste, foods, and teas generally do not interfere with remedy action, but you may want to avoid peppermint-flavored Altoids. Black, green, or herbal teas and other caffeinated drinks do not interfere with remedies.


What kinds of things may interfere with the action of homeopathic remedies?
Using substances with camphor such as Tiger Balm, Noxzema, Vicks, and Carmex lip balm; using tea tree oil; sleeping all night with electric blankets, heating pads, or heated waterbeds; exposing remedies to direct sunlight or extreme heat such as a closed car on a hot summer day or to strong X-rays such as those airlines use for checked baggage; touching remedies with your hands; or undergoing dental work with drilling and chemicals may interfere with the action or potency of homeopathic remedies. Be sure to store all homeopathic remedies away from microwave ovens, computers, TVs, and other electromagnetic devices. Talk with your practitioner if any of these factors may be affecting your response to homeopathy.


Do I have to be off all other medications or supplements for homeopathy to work?
No, homeopathy works at the energetic level, on a different plane from conventional medications or supplements, which work at the material level. Homeopathy will not interfere with any medications you are currently taking. It is important, however, to inform your homeopath of all current medications and supplements you are taking, so your remedy dosing can be adjusted to be most effective. You should not discontinue any prescribed medications without first consulting your doctor.


Will I have to keep seeing my homeopath frequently for an extended period of time or years?
This varies by individual. Homeopathy works by stimulating the body-mind to heal itself and come back into balance, and each person’s response to a remedy is unique. For best results with recurrent or chronic conditions, you should plan on seeing your practitioner for 30-60 minute follow-up appointments every 4-8 weeks for six months to one year. The purpose of these appointments is to ensure that your remedy is right for you, working well, and being taken at the optimal potency and frequency. After several months on a well-chosen remedy, clients are usually familiar enough with the subtle but powerful way homeopathy works and can simply schedule appointments as needed. If you have very complex and long-standing symptoms, you may need to continue to see your homeopath every 8-10 weeks until you feel such frequency is no longer necessary.

For acute conditions, such as influenza or an ear infection, a half-hour phone consult with your practitioner and a well-chosen remedy can produce results in minutes, hours, or days.
